A real world example using Wireshark

We will be using wireshark to see how the TCP three way handshake happens in the real world. So I have recorded a session of a website. For the legal purposes, I have removed the IP address of the website. And I have captured the data of my own browsing.

What is wireshark ?

If you know about it, you can skip this section. Wireshark is the tool to capture the HTTP data. You can record the usernames and passwords using the wireshark.

Let us begin!

I am running wireshark on the kali Linux running on the virtual box, and that's the reason, why you are viewing square screenshots.
When you click on a link, you are not just connecting to one server, you are connecting to many other servers. These servers are of adverts. So for the sake of simplicity, I went to the 192.168.0.1, which opens my router settings page.
Here you can see the SYN is set, but it has not sent the ACK. The server has sent the SYN and ACK The client sent an ACK, and the connection is established.

Does three way handshake happen in UDP connection?

TCP establishes a connection in order to provide reliability and flow control, which UDP doesn't. UDP is connectionless, which means that UDP doesn't perform three way handshake.
